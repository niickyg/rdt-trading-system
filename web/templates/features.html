Understanding Real Relative Strength

What is Real Relative Strength (RRS)?

RRS measures a stock's performance relative to the overall market (SPY), normalized by volatility. This identifies stocks that are genuinely outperforming or underperforming the market.

RRS = (Stock % Change - SPY % Change) / Stock ATR

Entry Criteria

  • RRS value exceeds threshold (typically > 2.0)
  • Daily chart confirms strength (above key EMAs)
  • ML model confidence > 72%
  • Favorable market regime
  • No negative news or upcoming earnings

Risk Management

  • Stop loss: 1x ATR below entry
  • Profit target: 2x ATR above entry
  • Position sizing: 2% account risk per trade
  • Maximum concurrent positions: 5
